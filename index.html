Methodology & assumptions

The calculator projects cash flows year-by-year with line-item expenses and planned events. Each year we add a safety margin, apply your net return (after tax & fees), deduct any retirement income, and carry the corpus forward. Stress testing reduces real return by −2% for the first 10 years after retirement. See the Audit for a worked row.

Formulas
Regular @ age A (from F to U, growth g): amountToday × (1+g)^(A − F), only if F ≤ A ≤ U

Planned @ event age E (inflation i): amountToday × (1+i)^(E − currentAge)

total = regular + planned
totalWithBuffer = total × (1 + safetyMargin)
retirementIncomeOffset = max(0, totalWithBuffer − retirementIncome)

effectiveRate(age) = net return (after tax & fees); stressed: −2% real for first 10 post-retirement years
return = startCorpus × effectiveRate(age)
endCorpus = startCorpus + return − retirementIncomeOffset
